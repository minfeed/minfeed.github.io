Thai Green Curry Chicken

A few extra touches on top of some curry paste to max out the flavour in this global top contender.
  • boneless skinless chicken breast or thigh
  • coconut oil
  • bell pepper
  • snap peas or green beans
  • can of bamboo shoots
  • lemongrass stalk
  • fish sauce
  • green curry paste
  • can of coconut milk
  • jasmine or basmati rice
  1. Cut the chicken into bite-sized slices and fry in a deep-walled frying pan or wok in coconut oil.
  2. Put water for the rice on to boil.
  3. Rice method 1: boil and strain like you would with pasta. Use a fine strainer. This method washes away a lot of the starch and there is no worry about water-to-rice ratio. Use enough water so that it does not boil dry.
  4. Rice method 2: two parts water to one part rice, bring to boil, cover and simmer for about 12-15 minutes (for white jasmine or basmati). Rinse the rice first to remove some starch.
  5. Slice bell pepper in strips and add to chicken.
  6. Trim the ends of the peas and/or beans and add to chicken.
  7. Drain the bamboo shoots and add.
  8. Cut the lemongrass stalk into a few pieces and add.
  9. Clear a spot in the middle of the pan and add a few big lumps of curry paste. Fry it up for a minute before mixing with everything else.
  10. Shake the coconut milk can really vigourously before opening to mix in the solidified bits. Pour in the pan, scraping out any remaining bits, and mix in.
  11. Add a few splashes of fish sauce, about a tablespoon.
  12. Simmer until the rice is ready.
  13. Plate up a bed of rice and scoop curry over top. The lemongrass pieces are not to eat, but are tasty to bite into.
