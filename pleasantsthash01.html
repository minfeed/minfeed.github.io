Pleasant St. Hash

Energy-rich belly-filler on the cheap.
  • rice
  • vermicelli
  • potatoes
  • baloney
  • cinammon
  • garlic powder
  • onion powder or salt
  • salt
  • pepper
  1. Put on a kettle of water on to boil.
  2. Put on a pot of water to boil the potatoes.
  3. Peel and dice about 4 or 5 potatoes and par-boil for about 8 minutes.
  4. Add some olive oil to a non-stick wok or deep-walled frying pan.
  5. Dice the baloney and add to the pan. Separate the baloney slices before dicing so the pieces don't stick together. Fry on medium heat, stirring often.
  6. Put a cup of rice in a sieve and soak and rinse in cold water for about a minute.
  7. In a pot, add a high smoke-point oil like canola or sunflower. Over medium-high heat, break about a twoonie-size handful of vermicelli into small pieces and fry. Turn often with a fork until most of the vermicelli turns brown.
  8. Add about 4 cups of the previously boiled water to the vermicelli. It will sizzle, steam and boil right away.
  9. Add the rice, a heaped tablespoon or so of cinammon, and salt to the vermicelli pot.
  10. Stir, reduce heat to medium low, cover and let simmer for about 10 minutes. Add more hot water if it starts to dry out before the rice is cooked.
  11. Strain the potatoes and add to the frying pan. Add more oil as needed to crisp up the potatoes and baloney.
  12. Add garlic powder, onion powder/salt, salt and pepper.
  13. Try to catch the rice just when all the water is almost absorbed. Remove from the heat, remove the lid, and let stand a few minutes.
  14. When the baloney and potatoes are crisped up enough, add the rice and vermicelli and mix all together.
