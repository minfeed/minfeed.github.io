Salsamole and Quesadillas

Fresh and light with a little kick.
  • tomatoes
  • salt
  • red onion
  • bell pepper
  • english cucmber
  • avacado
  • lime
  • cilantro
  • garlic
  • chili flakes or fresh chili
  • tortillas
  • cheddar or mozzeralla
  1. Preheat oven to 400 C.
  2. Dice two tomatoes, place in a strainer hanging in a bowl or pot, cover with salt and mix in. Set aside to let the liquid drain away. The salt helps release the liquid from the tomatoes.
  3. Finely dice a medium-sized red onion. Add less onion than the other ingredients. Add to a large bowl.
  4. Dice bell pepper and an equal amount of cucumber and add.
  5. Dice avacado and immediately cover with juice from a slice of lime to help prevent browning. Add to the bowl.
  6. Finely chop fresh cilantro and add.
  7. Chop two cloves of fresh garlic and add.
  8. Squeeze half a lime juice over everything.
  9. Add chili flakes or finely chopped fresh chili.
  10. Gently squish some more water out of the tomatoes and add.
  11. Mix everything together, trying to not smoosh up the avacado.
  12. On a baking sheet, grate cheese onto half of each tortilla and fold in half, then squish them flat.
  13. Bake about 8-10 minutes, until they start to get crispy.
  14. Serve each quesadilla with a huge scoop of salsamole.
