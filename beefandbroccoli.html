Beef and Broccoli

High in iron, an easy to replicate takeaway dish.
  • steak
  • sesame or coconut oil
  • salt
  • broccoli
  • rice
  • garlic
  • ginger, preferably fresh
  • broth, preferably beef
  • soy sauce
  • sesame seeds
  1. Put water for the rice on to boil.
  2. Rice method 1: boil and strain like you would with pasta. Use a fine strainer. This method washes away a lot of the starch and there is no worry about water-to-rice ratio. Use enough water so that it does not boil dry.
  3. Rice method 2: two parts water to one part rice, bring to boil, cover and simmer for about 12-15 minutes (for white jasmine or basmati). Rinse the rice first to remove some starch.
  4. Thinly slice beef and fry in coconut or sesame oil. Salt the beef.
  5. Cut broccoli into bite-size crowns and add to the beef.
  6. Finely chop and add a few cloves of garlic.
  7. Add fresh or ground ginger.
  8. Dissolve a heaped tablespoon of corn starch in about a half cup of stock and add to the pan.
  9. Add more stock to get the desired consistency. The corn starch will thicken as it cooks.
  10. Pour in some soy sauce.
  11. Garnish with sesame seeds.
