Masala Curry Chicken

Masala means spice mixture.
  • onion
  • olive oil, coconut oil, or ghee
  • carrot
  • sweet pepper
  • garlic
  • tomatoes, fresh or canned
  • chicken broth
  • chicken
  • cardamom seeds
  • fennel seeds
  • coriander seeds
  • cloves
  • peppercorns
  • chili flakes
  • turmeric
  • cinnamon
  • coconut milk
  1. Put a kettle of water on to boil.
  2. In a large saucepan, saute a diced onion in olive or coconut oil. Use ghee aka clarified butter if you want to go next level.
  3. Add diced sweet pepper and carrot.
  4. Add a few cloves of finely chopped garlic.
  5. Add several diced tomatoes, or a can of diced or whole tomatoes.
  6. Add a cup or more of chicken broth.
  7. Add enough boiled water to cover the veg and create enough volume of sauce.
  8. After the veg are soft, blend smooth with an immersion blender, and let simmer.
  9. In a separate pan or wok large enough to hold all the sauce, fry bite-size pieces of chicken in olive or coconut oil or ghee.
  10. Make the spice mixture, grinding seeds in a mortar and pestle. Use dried spices if you can't grind fresh. Cardamom seeds, fennel seeds, coriander seeds, cloves, peppercorn, chili flakes, turmeric, cinnamon. Or use the garam masala powder or paste from here
  11. When the chicken is browned, clear an area in the middle of the pan and fry the spices.
  12. Add the sauce from the other pan.
  13. Add a can of coconut milk.
  14. Serve over basmati or jasmine rice.
