Fried Rice

Use up leftover protein and veg in this stir-fry in disguise.
  • rice
  • onion
  • olive, coconut, or sesame oil
  • mushrooms
  • chicken, beef, pork, or tofu
  • fresh garlic and/or ginger
  • carrot
  • sweet pepper
  • curry powder
  • paprika
  • turmeric
  • cumin
  • 2 or 3 eggs
  • soy sauce
  • spring onions
  • cilantro
  • sesame seeds
  • sriracha or other hot sauce
  1. Put water for the rice on to boil.
  2. Rice method 1: boil and strain like you would with pasta. Use a fine strainer. This method washes away a lot of the starch and there is no worry about water-to-rice ratio. Use enough water so that it does not boil dry.
  3. Rice method 2: two parts water to one part rice, bring to boil, cover and simmer for about 12-15 minutes (for white jasmine or basmati). Rinse the rice first to remove some starch.
  4. Dice and onion and fry in olive, coconut, or sesame oil.
  5. Finely chop mushrooms into tiny little pieces so the kids won't know they're there, and add to the onions.
  6. If using fresh uncooked protein, cut into bit-size pieces and cook through.
  7. Add finely chopped garlic and ginger.
  8. Grate in a carrot.
  9. Add a finely chopped sweet pepper.
  10. When the rice is done and ready to be added, first add the spices. Clear a little area in the middle of the pan and add fresh oil. Fry any or all of curry powder, paprika, powdered garlic and ginger if you didn't use fresh, turmeric, cumin. Mix throroughly with all the veg.
  11. In a separate frying pan, cook up two or three scrambled eggs.
  12. Add the rice to the veg mixture and stir until the rice is evenly coated.
  13. Add a few splashes of soy sauce.
  14. Add the eggs and stir throroughly.
  15. Top with thinly sliced spring onions, cilantro, sesame seeds and sriracha sauce.
