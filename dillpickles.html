Dill Pickles

Small batch size that can be water bathed on the stove top. Makes 4 jars.
  • english cucumbers
  • 3 parts distilled or filtered water
  • 1 part pickling vinegar 5% acid
  • pickling or non-iodized coarse salt
  • sugar
  • garlic
  • fresh dill
  • optionally small chilis
  1. Clean and rinse the jars and lids.
  2. In a pot large enough to cover the jars, bring a full pot of tap water to boil.
  3. In a separate pot, prepare the brine. 6 cups filtered water, 2 cups vinegar, 1/2 cup pickling salt, 2 tbsp sugar. Bring to boil.
  4. Boil a kettle of tap water for topping up the water bath later.
  5. 3 large english cucumbers just about fills four jars. Cut lengths to fit jars, then cut into wedges.
  6. Place jars in the boiling water for a few minutes, drain, and rest on a towel.
  7. Add the cucumber spears to the jars, packing just tight without squishing them.
  8. Add a partially crushed garlic clove to each jar.
  9. Set aside a sprig of dill for each jar, then finely chop the rest and divide between the jars.
  10. Pour boiling brine into jars, filling to 1/2" from the top.
  11. Insert a dill sprig into each jar. Sterilize the tool you use in the boiling water first.
  12. Tap the jars or use a sterilized tool to remove any air bubbles.
  13. Place the lids and tops in the boiling water for a few minutes, then remove and place on towel.
  14. Water bath. Wipe the top of the jars, place on the lids, and screw on the tops just finger tight so the air can escape. Place the jars in the boiling water, keeping the jar upright so the liquid inside does not touch the rim. Top up with more boiling water from the kettle if needed.
  15. Return to rolling boil then boil for 10 minutes. This will be as short a water bath as possible to avoid cooking the cucumbers too long, yet still be safe enough to kill all bacteria.
  16. Remove from the water bath, again keeping upright so the liquid does not touch the rim, and let cool for 12 hours without touching.
  17. Store in a cool location, and wait 2 weeks before opening.
