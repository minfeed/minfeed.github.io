Tao Chicken Salad

  • boneless skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • red onion
  • bell pepper
  • snap peas
  • cherry tomatoes
  • lettuce and/or spinach
  • avacado
  • lettuce
  • cilantro
  • spring onions
  • lime
  • garlic
  • ginger
  • corn starch or flour
  • cumin
  • paprika
  • turmeric
  • salt
  • pepper
  • chili flakes
  • fresh noodles or dry ramen noodles
  • sesame oil
  • coconut or olive oil
  • balsamic vinegar
  • sesame seeds
  1. Prep everything first because once you start cooking it will come together fast. Cut most things into fork-sized bites. Finely chop the garlic and ginger. Roughy chop the cilantro. Finely slice the spring onions.
  2. In a bowl big enough to toss the chicken, mix corn starch or flour (~2 Tbsps), cumin, paprika, turmeric, salt, pepper, & chili flakes. Toss the chicken until coated.
  3. Put water for noodles on to boil.
  4. Fry chicken and red onion in coconut or olive oil.
  5. For the salad dressing, in a small bowl or jar, mix sesame oil, lime juice, balsamic vinegar.
  6. Put noodles in to boil, timed so they are ready at the same time as the chicken.
  7. Toss finely chopped garlic and ginger in with the chicken with a good dollop of sesame oil about two minutes before the noodles are ready.
  8. Strain the noodles, turn off the heat on the chicken, and toss the noddles in the pan with the chicken and onions.
  9. Make a bed of lettuce on a serving plate.
  10. Spread noodles and chicken over the lettuce.
  11. Cover with peppers, cherry tomatoes, and snap peas. Arrange avacado slices around the edges. Sprinkle cilantro, spring onions, and sesame seeds over top. Pour the dressing evenly over everything.
