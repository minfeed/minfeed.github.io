Baked Chicken Parm and Pilau Rice

Get the same crunchy-gooey texture by swapping the breading with crumbled croutons, and baking the rice.
  • boneless skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • can of tomato sauce
  • croutons
  • cheddar or mozerella cheese
  • garlic, oregano or italian spice, chili flakes, pepper
  • rice
  • onion
  • turmeric, paprika, garlic
  1. Preheat oven to 400Â°F.
  2. Brown both sides of the chicken in a pan then place in a casserole dish.
  3. Using the same pan, heat tomato sauce, adding garlic, oregano or italian seasoning, chili flakes, and pepper.
  4. Cover the chicken in the casserole dish with the sauce. Crumble croutons over top, then cover with grated cheese.
  5. Cover with aluminum foil to prevent the cheese from browning too soon and place in the oven.
  6. Put a kettle of water on to boil.
  7. Saute diced onion.
  8. Soak 1.5 cups of rice in water to rinse off the starch, then strain.
  9. Smear olive oil over the bottom and sides of a deep-walled baking pan.
  10. To the onion add turmeric, paprika, and garlic.
  11. Add the rice to the pan and mix thoroughly with the onion-spice mix so the rice is fully coated.
  12. Spread the rice mixture evenly in the baking pan, and gently cover with 3.5 cups of the hot water. Try not to wash the spice coating around too much.
  13. Cover with aluminum foil or a cover, trying to seal the moisture in a best as possible, and place in the oven for about 30 minutes.
  14. After chicken has been in for about 20 minutes, remove it's aluminum foil so the cheese will brown, and bake for another 20 minutes.
  15. Let the chicken dish rest for at least 5 minutes before serving. Fluff the rice with a fork, scraping the crunchy bits off the side of the pan.
