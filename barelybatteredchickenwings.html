Barely Battered Chicken Wings

Trimmed down and dressed up, sweet and spicy, crispy and sticky all in one.
  • chicken wings
  • corn starch or flour
  • cumin
  • paprika
  • curry powder
  • poultry seasoning
  • rosemary
  • thyme
  • garlic powder
  • salt
  • pepper
  • honey
  • soy sauce
  • chili flakes or sriracha
  • garlic
  • ginger
  • lime juice
  1. Trim as much skin as possible off the wings. Place a knife against the edge of the wing and pull the skin under the knife, keeping the knife straight up, perpindicular to the cutting board. Let the wing roll as the skin is pulled.
  2. In a bowl big enough to toss the chicken, mix corn starch or flour (~2 Tbsps/dozen wings), cumin, paprika, cury powder, poultry seasoning, rosemary, thyme, garlic powder, salt, pepper. Toss the chicken until coated.
  3. Deep fry or bake the wings until fully cooked.
  4. While wings are cooking, in a saucepan slowly bring honey, soy sauce, sriracha, lime juice, garlic and ginger to a boil.
  5. Put the wings in a large bowl, douse them in the sauce, and toss to coat. The light dusting batter will start to absorb the sauce, so eat them quick while they retain a little crunch.
